Fill-up for Hope

As part of my assignment, I developed a community program called Fill-up for Hope, which provides gas cards to local cancer patients. The initiative’s primary fundraiser was the Cancer Awareness Banquet. I partnered with the local cancer institute, which agreed to distribute the cards to patients, created sponsorship and donation documents, secured sponsorships from local businesses. I also designed and developed the program’s website—creating the content, writing the code, and producing the initial layout in Adobe XD. The site was built to be fully responsive and user-friendly. This program was a great help in our community and the program was adopted by several other churches in the area.

about me

I started in the tech field many years ago while working as an executive assistant to the Chief Information Officer. The CIO encouraged me to take IT courses and allowed me to use my newly learned skills on the job. First I learned to build computers, then desktop support, networking, then databases, and so on.

This fusion of tech and administrative skills has been a great benefit to me over the years. Though it may appear that I am exploring a new field; it is more accurate to say that I am continuing to expand my knowledge in Information Technology. For me, expanding into front-end development is a natural progression and fit well with my background and my innate desire to continually learn new things.
